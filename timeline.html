Feb 20, 2023

LEARNS Act introduced

Lawmakers file a bill incorporating Gov. Sarah Huckabee Sanders’ education overhaul for Arkansas. The bill encompasses several initiatives, including increasing teachers' salaries, literacy rate improvements, and school voucher programs.

March 9, 2023

LEARNS ACT signed into law

Sanders signs the LEARNS Act into law following a three-week process where the bill was reviewed by the state Legislature. The governor describes the law as “a comprehensive approach to all education, from cradle to career." However, some critics argued that the process to pass the bill was rushed.

May 26, 2023

Judge blocks implmentation of LEARNS

A Pulaski County circuit judge blocks implementation of the LEARNS Act until Aug. 1. Arkansas Attorney General Tim Griffin files an immediate notice of appeal over Wright's temporary restraining order to the Arkansas Supreme Court. The judge’s ruling blocks the failing Marvell-Elaine School District from entering an agreement with FEF-Arkansas to run its day-to-day operations.

July 18, 2023

Emergency rules allow families to apply for vouchers

State lawmakers approve emergency rules to allow families to apply and access voucher funding for private schools while the state waits for the LEARNS Act to be formally implemented on Aug. 1st.

Aug. 1, 2023

LEARNS Act takes effect

Pending any additional litigation, implementation of phase one of the LEARNS Act will begin. Educational Freedom Accounts, a voucher program that allows families to use state funding to send their children to private or home schools, will be open to about 7,000 eligible students. By the 2025-2026 school year, it will be open to all students.
